Cadence of
Broken Night

By Joshua Yang

& these empty tears—
I wish they could flood
over me & bury me in a swirl of black and blue
& then perhaps I’d call it
my own constellation.

[Ghost light: the phenomenon of light from
distant stars taking hundreds upon thousands
of years to reach the naked eye on Earth.]

I know: the night sky doesn’t sing for me
anymore, these two ticking clocks in
a field of forgotten / forsaken euphoria.

[Due to the finite speed of light, it’s reasonable
to assume somewhere out there lies a long-
gone star only existing in our own darkness.]

let’s put the past behind us,
you and I—
& find something new
to reminisce over—

& when we’re done, we’ll put it back
in our heartwood box,
closed shut with fitful sleep
from this ragged-edged exterior.

Believe me when I say this:
we’re meant to be here,
shaped by / in spite of / past
this sublime trauma

& up there, scattered
among a sea of broken stars
maybe we’ll find a piece of myself
not yet undone.
